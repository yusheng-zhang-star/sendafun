1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) by a website you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, by remembering your actions and preferences (such as login, language, or session state) so you don't have to re-enter them every time you come back to the site or browse from one page to another. Cookies may be set by the site you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third-party domains that run content or services embedded on the site ("third-party cookies").

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as web beacons (also called clear gifs or pixel tags), local storage / IndexedDB, and device fingerprinting signals. These technologies help us count visits, understand usage patterns, measure engagement, and detect abuse. For simplicity, we refer to all of these technologies collectively as "cookies" throughout this policy unless the context requires otherwise.

When you first visit sendafun.com, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or reject non-essential categories of cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" link in the footer or by adjusting the settings in your browser. Your current consent choice is stored locally on your device for up to 12 months.

3. Analytics (GA4, anonymized)

Off by default · Requires consent

With your consent, we use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to help us understand how visitors interact with our website and the Service. GA4 collects pseudonymous data such as the pages you visit, the links you click, how long you stay on a page, what browser and device you are using, your approximate geographic region (derived from IP address, truncated to country or city level), and your traffic source. We have configured GA4 to anonymize IP addresses of all visitors, meaning the last octet of your IP address is dropped immediately upon collection and never stored in Google's logs. We do not use any other Google advertising features in combination with GA4.

5. How to disable or manage cookies

Most modern web browsers provide settings that allow you to control cookies — you can choose to block all cookies, accept only certain categories, or delete existing cookies. The exact steps depend on your browser; please follow the links below for instructions for the most popular browsers. Please note that if you choose to block or delete essential cookies, certain features of the Service may stop working or become unreliable, and you may need to re-enter your credentials more frequently.

Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Third-party cookies & Site settings → Cookies (or visit chrome://settings/cookies)
Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Browser Privacy → Cookies and Site Data
Safari (macOS): Settings → Privacy → Cookies and website data → Manage Website Data
Safari (iOS): Settings → Safari → Block Cookies
Microsoft Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and site permissions
Brave: Settings → Shields → Cookie controls → Cookie blocking

For Google Analytics specifically, you can install the official Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on for your desktop browser at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout, which prevents the GA4 JavaScript from sending data about your visit to Google Analytics on any website that uses it. For mobile users can also enable your browser's built-in Privacy Badging and blocking capability (e.g. Apple App Tracking Transparency on iOS).
